OUR STORY

Welcome to our family owned restaurant, our menu features seasonally driven dishes with fresh flavours. We're all about appealing and flavoursome food that is perfect for finer dining. Our chefs celebrate the highest quality of local ingredients in every dish and support local and regional producers. We are proud of our reputation as ‘the’ place for breakfast in Port Macquarie with a vibrant selection of wholesome and healthy fare to brighten your day. We also know how important great coffee is and our experienced baristas proudly serve Campos Coffee from 7am till close 7 days a week. Inspired by authentic flavour combinations, our menu offers a relaxed but sophisticated dining option. Our Kangaroo Loin is seared with horseradish & dijon mayonnaise, crunchy baby capers, beetroot dust and micro herbs. We uncork the best & brightest with a drinks menu that mixes crowd favourites with boutique drops. We offer wines by the glass and an interesting mix of local craft and imported beers.

Our Journey

Starting a restaurant is a dream for many, but the journey to making that dream a reality is far from easy. Just ask Jake, a young restaurateur who decided to take the plunge and open his own place, who quickly learned that the road to success is paved with obstacles and challenges, from scraping together funding to dealing with unexpected challenges, to hiring the right management team, the first year of running a restaurant can be a wild ride, but with the right attitude, hard work, and determination, it can also be incredibly rewarding. In this article, we're sharing Jake's story to give you a behind-the-scenes look at what it takes to make it in the restaurant game and to help you prepare for the journey ahead. When Jake first decided to open his own restaurant, he knew it wouldn't be easy. But he never could have imagined just how many obstacles he would have to overcome. First and foremost, there was the issue of funding. Traditional bank loans were out of the question, and Jake didn't have any rich investors to turn to. So, he did what any determined entrepreneur would do - he used his own savings to get the ball rolling.
